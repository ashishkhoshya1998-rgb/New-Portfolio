## Filing was broken at scale

India's GST regime requires over **1.4 crore businesses** to file GSTR-3B returns monthly or quarterly — reporting sales, ITC claims, tax liabilities, and refunds. The GSTN portal limited every user to a single GSTIN at a time. An enterprise with 120 GSTINs ran the same painful workflow 120 times. Manual data entry, opaque reconciliation, and Excel dependency compounded at every step.

<PullQuote attribution="CFO, Manufacturing Firm">
My team of 6 spends 5 full days every month filing for 120 GSTINs. We make mistakes because the process creates mistakes.
</PullQuote>

Five failure points compounded at every step:

- **High cognitive load** — manual tracking that scaled exponentially with GSTIN count
- **Limited automation** — 82% of users copying from Excel because copy-paste was restricted
- **Reconciliation breakage** — 65% of support tickets were ITC mismatches
- **Filing-flow abandonment** — a standalone "data source" gate caused 47% drop-off
- **Excel dependency** — pivots, filters, and bulk actions all happened off-platform

## What 50+ interviews revealed

I shadowed MSMEs, large enterprise finance teams, and tax consultants while they filed. I synthesised support ticket trends and ran usability tests on Clear GST 1.0 and the GSTN portal to triangulate what was breaking and where.

## Configuration in context, not isolation

Each pain mapped to a design principle: **bulk filing**, **automated reconciliation**, **data-source flexibility**, **in-platform Excel parity**, and **Table-4 transparency**. Here's how each one shipped — starting with the data source layer.

I designed it around a simple premise: users shouldn't have to learn what GSTR-2B is before they can file. Table-specific dropdown mapping with smart auto-suggested defaults handled the cognitive lift; a preview mode let users validate auto-filled values before committing.

## One dashboard for 120 GSTINs

The biggest behavioural shift was getting users out of single-GSTIN tunnel vision. A centralised dashboard surfaced filing status, tax liabilities, and ITC across every GSTIN — with batch filing for 10+ in one click. Enterprises activated 20+ GSTINs in under 10 minutes.

## Reconciliation as a glass box

Reconciliation was the second-most-cited pain point and the source of most support tickets. I built a split-view comparison of GSTR-2B vs Purchase Register with mismatches highlighted, and bulk approve/reject for 100+ invoices in one click. Vendor grouping and partial invoice matching followed post-launch.

## Table-4, you can trust

Users didn't trust auto-generated values because they couldn't see how reconciliation decisions affected their ITC. We considered three options: tooltip-per-cell, a side panel with live preview, or a post-fill summary. The live side panel won — it shows cause and effect as decisions are made, building trust through transparency.

## Adoption ≠ Proficiency

We launched. Metrics looked good. Then I started shadowing real users.

The 90-90-90 adoption drive — 90% of users using 90% of features within 90 days — was being incentivised through Customer Success Managers. CSMs were hitting their targets. But monthly retros revealed something the dashboards couldn't see: CSMs were doing tasks *for* users to hit those targets. Users were filing but not mastering the platform.

We pivoted from CSM-led metrics to direct user shadowing — open-ended questions like *"How would you solve this without the platform?"* — and SQL funnel analysis to find the real drop-off points. Three issues surfaced almost immediately:

<ProcessStep number="01" title="The data source page was a barrier">
  Users saw the standalone data source setup as a "gatekeeper," not a workflow enabler. <em>"Why can't I adjust sources directly in the GSTR-3B form?"</em> 47% dropped off or called CSMs to bypass it.
</ProcessStep>

<ProcessStep number="02" title="Table-4 was opaque">
  Users still couldn't see how reconciliation actions impacted Table-4. <em>"Your platform's logic is a black box."</em> 89% reverted to Excel.
</ProcessStep>

<ProcessStep number="03" title="Excel dependency persisted">
  Native filtering, grouping, and bulk actions were missing — users downloaded data even after launch.
</ProcessStep>

<ActivityTags tags={["Field Shadowing", "SQL Funnel Analysis", "Open-Ended Research"]} />

## Fixing what shadowing revealed

Four iterative fixes — small structural changes with disproportionate UX impact.

**1. Data source moved inline.** The standalone "gatekeeper" step disappeared. Source configuration now lives inside the GSTR-3B form — users adjust while reviewing Table-4. Drop-off: 47% → near zero.

![Iteration: Data source controls moved inline — from blocker to enabler](/cs/gstr-datasrc-redesign.gif)
*The configuration step that blocked 47% of users — moved inline into the form they were already filling.*

**2. Transparent Table-4 workflow.** Real-time ITC impact previews — every reconciliation row updates the final Table-4 value live. Black box became glass box.

![Iteration: Table-4 live preview — every reconciliation action updates ITC in real time](/cs/gstr-table4.gif)
*Every reconciliation action updates Table-4 in real time — the same insight, surfaced when it matters.*

**3. Excel-free grouping.** Native grouping and subgrouping replaced Excel pivot tables entirely — efficient, accurate bulk actions in-platform.

![Iteration: Native grouping eliminates Excel dependency](/cs/gstr-excel-free.gif)
*Native grouping for bulk actions — no more pivot-table round-trips.*

**4. Multi-GSTIN auto-OTP.** Instant GSTIN activation with automated OTP capture (with consent) — filing time dropped from 30 min to under 2 min per batch.

## Three lessons that outlived the project

**Adoption ≠ Proficiency.** Incentivising usage without ensuring understanding backfires. Dashboards reported green; field shadowing showed users were filing but not mastering. The lesson stuck: trust user behaviour, not CSM metrics.

**Context beats isolation.** Moving the data source step *inside* the GSTR-3B form — not before it — was a small structural change with massive UX impact. Where something lives in a flow shapes how users understand it.

**Transparency builds trust.** Users need to see how their actions shape outcomes. Live Table-4 previews didn't just clarify the math — they drove adoption where mandates had failed. The same insight, surfaced at the right moment.
